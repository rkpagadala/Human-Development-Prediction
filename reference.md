Lutz W (2009). "Sola schola et sanitate: Human Capital as the Root Cause and Priority for International Development." Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B: Biological Sciences 364(1532): 3031–3047.
  - Core argument: human capital (education + health) is the root cause of development; income is an intermediate outcome, not the driver.
  - Title echoes Protestant "sola scriptura" — just as the Reformation held salvation flows only through reading, development flows only through school and health.
  - Directly relevant to: the Protestant-Catholic literacy divergence (UK/Germany/Sweden vs Spain/Portugal visible in WCDE cohort data back to 1875), the "initial income dominates" finding in our T+25 GDP regression (which reflects centuries of compounded education advantage misread as income persistence), and the policy implication that education investment must precede income growth rather than wait for it.
